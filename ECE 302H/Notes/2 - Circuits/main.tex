\section{System}

Everything can be a system. A system just needs to have a form of input,
whether that is doing something or not, and an output, which is just 
some reaction that we care about.

In a circuit specifically, inputs are usually independent sources, and
outputs are any $v$, or $i$ of interest anywhere in the circuit.

\begin{remark}
"System" is a very broad category and the one form of system that we are
particularly interested in right now is "Linear Systems" or linearity.
\end{remark}

\section{Linearity}

\begin{definition}
An equation is linear if it takes the follonwg form: $C_y y^1 = C_u u^1$
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}
A SISO (single input single output) system is defined as:

\begin{align*}
y &= f(u) \\
u &\Rightarrow y
\end{align*}

For some specific input $u_1$, $u_1 \Rightarrow y_1$.

$y = f(u)$ is linear if, for any constants $a$, $b$

\begin{equation*}
f(au_1 + bu_2) = af(u_1) + bf(u_2)  
\end{equation*}
\end{definition}

Going back to the first definition,

\begin{align*}
C_y y &= C_u u \\
y &= \frac{C_u}{C_y} u \\
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
f(a u_1 + b u_2) &= \frac{C_u}{C_y}(a u_1 + b u_2) \\
&= a \left( \frac{C_u}{C_y} u_1 \right) + b \left( \frac{C_u}{C_y} u_2 \right) \\
&= a f(u_1) + b f(u_2)
\end{align*}
